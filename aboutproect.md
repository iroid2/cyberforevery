## 1. Executive Summary

CyberSecurity for Everyone (cyber4every1) is a Chicago-based educational technology initiative founded by Ivan Zziwa. The organization's core mission is to make cybersecurity education accessible, practical, and engaging for every individual — regardless of age, background, or technical experience.

The flagship product is a 4-week online and in-person **Cybersecurity & AI Bootcamp for teens in Grades 8–12**. The platform is being expanded into a full learning ecosystem that includes student dashboards, parent portals, email automation, assessments, and a multi-course curriculum roadmap.

## 5. Bootcamp Program Details

### Flagship: Cybersecurity & AI Bootcamp for Teens (Grades 8–12)

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4 Weeks |
| **Schedule** | 2 sessions per week, 1.5 hours each |
| **Total Hours** | 12 hours of instruction + optional Friday office hours (2 hrs/week) |
| **Format** | Online and In-Person options available |
| **Target Audience** | Teens in Grades 8–12 (ages ~13–18) |
| **Class Size** | Small groups for personalized support |
| **Certification** | Certificate of Completion issued upon finishing |

### 4-Week Curriculum

**Week 1 — Foundations & Setup**
- Day 1: Intro to cybersecurity & AI. Tool setup: VS Code, GitHub, ChatGPT.
- Day 2: How computers and the internet work. IP addresses, DNS, routers.

**Week 2 — Cyber Safety**
- Day 3: Passwords, authentication, multi-factor auth.
- Day 4: Phishing, scams, and how to identify and avoid online threats.

**Week 3 — AI in Action**
- Day 5: Using AI tools responsibly and effectively.
- Day 6: AI risks, deepfakes, misinformation, and digital ethics.

**Week 4 — Build & Present**
- Day 7: Final project work session.
- Day 8: Demo Day — students present projects to the class. 🎤

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of the bootcamp, students will be able to:
- Understand core cybersecurity principles and safe online practices
- Explain how computers and the internet function
- Identify and respond to common cyber threats (phishing, weak passwords, social engineering)
- Use AI tools like ChatGPT safely and effectively
- Complete and present a hands-on final project

## 9. Student Weekly Routine

Every student follows this repeating weekly loop throughout the 4-week bootcamp:

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| **Monday** | Attend live session (1.5 hrs). New concepts introduced. Tools and activities completed in class. |
| **Mon–Wed** | Complete homework assignment (e.g., create 3 strong passwords, identify 5 phishing signs). Logged in student dashboard. |
| **Wednesday** | Attend second live session (1.5 hrs). Build on Monday's concepts. Group discussion and hands-on activity. |
| **Wed–Fri** | Weekly assessment unlocks on the student dashboard. Student submits answers at their own pace. |
| **Friday** | Optional 2-hour office hours with Ivan. Q&A, extra help, project feedback. |
| **Weekend** | Parent receives weekly progress email. Next week's curriculum unlocks on Sunday night. |
